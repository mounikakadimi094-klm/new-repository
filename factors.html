Beyond Diet: The Complex Causes

Physical growth is not just about calorie intake; it’s shaped by a confluence of socio-economic and environmental factors.

  1. Socio-Economic Barriers

    Poverty, low literacy rates, and livelihood instability lead to chronic food insecurity and limited health expenditure.

  2. Environmental Isolation

    Geographical remoteness makes access to functioning primary healthcare centers and clean, potable water difficult.

  3. Disease Burden

    Higher prevalence of infectious diseases (e.g., malaria, diarrheal diseases) compromises nutrient absorption, directly inhibiting growth.

Understanding the root causes is the first step toward effective change.
