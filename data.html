Anthropometric Indices: Stunting, Wasting, and BMI

Physical growth is measured using Z-scores based on WHO reference standards. These metrics provide a clear picture of nutritional status.

Key Indicators

  • Stunting (Height-for-Age): Reflects *chronic* under-nutrition and long-term failure to grow. Tribal populations often show a high burden of stunting.
  • Wasting (Weight-for-Height): Reflects *acute* under-nutrition or recent, severe weight loss.
  • Underweight (Weight-for-Age): A composite measure reflecting both chronic and acute deficits.

The Data Gap

While regional data exists, comprehensive, nationally standardized growth charts specifically for diverse tribal groups are often limited, making targeted interventions challenging.
